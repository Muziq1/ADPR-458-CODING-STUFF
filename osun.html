Sacred:
Voices from the Osun River

A documentary about faith, pollution, and the press

For a decade, journalists investigated the pollution of Nigeria's sacred Osun River. Did their coverage help the communities who needed it most — or did it fail them?

What the film found

A decade of journalism, examined

What worked

Coverage created pressure

International reporting — particularly from BBC and Reuters — succeeded in elevating the story to global attention and placing pressure on government to respond publicly.

What failed

The wrong voices led

Much coverage consulted scientists and officials before community and spiritual leaders — inverting the hierarchy of who carries authority over a sacred site.

What was missed

Rhetoric caused harm

Some reporting's language around the river's danger deterred worshippers from sacred ceremonies — an unintended consequence that no journalist accounted for.
